Author Background (Fyodor Dostoevsky)

Fyodor Dostoevsky (1821-1881) was a Russian novelist, short story writer, essayist, and journalist. His works explore human psychology in the troubled political, social, and spiritual atmosphere of 19th-century Russia. He is considered one of the greatest and most influential novelists of all time. His major works include "Crime and Punishment," "The Brothers Karamazov," "The Idiot," and "Notes from Underground."

About the Story

"Kuttavum Shikshayum" (Crime and Punishment) tells the story of Rodion Raskolnikov, an impoverished former student in Saint Petersburg, who formulates a plan to kill an unscrupulous pawnbroker for her money. Raskolnikov justifies his actions with a theory that some individuals are extraordinary and thus beyond moral law. The novel delves into Raskolnikov's psychological turmoil, his struggle with guilt, and his eventual path to redemption. It explores the social and moral complexities of 19th-century Russia, examining themes of poverty, crime, and the human conscience. The novel is a profound exploration of the human psyche, examining the consequences of moral transgression and the possibility of spiritual renewal.
