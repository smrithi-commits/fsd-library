Author Background

Here's a summary of his author background: Family and Education: He was born on April 27, 1933, and passed away on October 3, 1999. He was the son of the renowned Malayalam writer Lalithambika Antharjanam and Narayanan Namboodiri. He obtained a degree in the Sciences and later earned an MA in Malayalam literature from University College, Thiruvananthapuram.

About story

Orikkal" (ഒരിക്കൽ), by N. Mohanan, stands as a poignant and deeply introspective work within Malayalam literature. This novel, initially serialized as "Ragangalkku Oru Kaalam" in Malayala Manorama, transcends conventional romantic narratives, evolving into a profound exploration of human relationships, memory, and the lingering echoes of love. At its core, "Orikkal" is marked by its autobiographical undertones. N. Mohanan draws from his own life, particularly a significant romantic relationship, to craft a narrative that resonates with raw emotional honesty. This personal dimension imbues the story with a sense of authenticity, allowing readers to connect with the characters and their experiences on a deeply human level. The novel is characterized by its nuanced exploration of love, not as a fleeting emotion, but as a force that shapes and defines lives. It delves into the complexities of human connection, portraying the delicate interplay of emotions, desires, and vulnerabilities that characterize intimate relationships. The narrative captures the ephemeral nature of such connections, acknowledging the inevitability of loss and the enduring impact of cherished memories. N. Mohanan's writing style is a hallmark of "Orikkal." His prose is marked by its lyrical quality, evoking a sense of nostalgia and introspection. He employs evocative language and imagery to create a rich emotional landscape, allowing readers to immerse themselves in the characters' experiences. The narrative unfolds with a measured pace, allowing for moments of quiet reflection and emotional depth.
